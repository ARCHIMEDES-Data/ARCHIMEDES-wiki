⚠️ Data submitted to [ARCHIMEDES](https://www.archimedesdata.ca/) **must** comply with applicable privacy regulations and ethical approvals. 

➡️ At a **minimum**, all data must be **coded** and have the necessary permissions for sharing.  
➡️ All **direct identifiers** (e.g. names, social insurance numbers) are removed and replaced with a unique code, prior to contribution.   
➡️ Data can be shared if it is **de-identified** (or local equivalent). This requires that all direct identifiers and most indirect identifiers must be removed or transformed to reduce the risk of re-identification to a very low level, in accordance with recognized standards and applicable legal and ethical requirements.
  
⚠️ **Researchers are responsible for ensuring their data is prepared appropriately.**  
➡️ For more information, please refer to the [De Identification Resources Hub](https://www.archimedesdata.ca/resources/data-de-identification/) and the [Deidentification Tutorials](https://github.com/ARCHIMEDES-Data). This includes structuring the data so it can be processed correctly by ARCHIMEDES (i.e., how the system organizes, validates, and imports data).

💡**Tip**: Health data may contain direct identifiers (e.g., names, health card numbers, exact addresses), indirect identifiers (e.g., date of birth, postal code, rare diagnoses), or embedded identifiers (e.g., metadata in imaging files or text within images). These elements must be carefully reviewed and either removed or modified to protect privacy.  
  
➡️ According to [ARCHIMEDES Data Submission](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1gpxSvGPcu3ioSSPNFqOJJtC5vvxRFu2t/edit), **Date of Birth** (DOB) is required as it supports data validation, structuring, and analysis (e.g., age consistency and longitudinal tracking). While the full format **MM-DD-YYYY** is preferred, users may alternatively provide **MM-YYYY** or **YYYY** when full DOB is not available or cannot be shared.  
➡️ If the year is not available, users may provide **age** only. In these cases, the system applies a default setting that calculates a standardized DOB based on the available information (month/year or age) to ensure consistency within the database structure, while still treating DOB as an indirect identifier and maintaining privacy protections.

#### Data preparation

The ARCHIMEDES clinical pipeline **automates** the process of importing REDCap study data into ARCHIMEDES. It handles instrument installation, candidate and session creation, and data ingestion in a single run.
The following must be set up in ARCHIMEDES before running the data ingestion pipeline:  

* **Project** –  Your study project must already exist in ARCHIMEDES (e.g., "CNMDP").  
* **Site** – The site where data was collected must already exist in LORIS (e.g., "University of Ottawa Heart Institute", “The Royal/IMHR”).  
* **Cohort** – The participant cohort must exist and be linked to the project (e.g., “Affected,” “Not-Affected,” “Control”)  
* **Visits** –  Each REDCap event (e.g., visit_1_arm_1, visit_2_arm_1) must be registered as a visit in LORIS and linked to the project and cohort. The pipeline does not create visit definitions automatically.   

All of the above will be created by the ARCHIMEDES technical team on the database.

#### Data transfer 

To prepare data for ingestion into ARCHIMEDES, the data must be structured according to the specifications defined by the [ARCHIMEDES ingestion pipelines](https://github.com/aces/archimedes-pipelines) (available as a reference). Each data type (e.g., imaging, behavioural, biospecimen...) must be prepared separately and follow the specific formatting requirements for that data type.

Data ingestion refers to the process of importing and organizing the data within the ARCHIMEDES platform so that it can be securely stored, managed, and accessed. This step is managed by the CNMDP and ARCHIMEDES technical teams and is therefore transparent to the user. No action is required from the user at this stage.

#### UOHI Data De-identification control and transfer process

The following section outlines the process for transferring data to ARCHIMEDES through UOHI. The workflow involves coordination between the Principal Investigator (PI), ARCHIMEDES, and UOHI teams to ensure data is securely reviewed, de-identified, and ingested.

<img width="816" height="1056" alt="Copy - ARCHIMEDES UOHI Data Transfer Workflow (4)" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d60210b4-990a-4b4c-9c64-3f62953e4e96" />

➡️ **Step 1: Project Setup (PI) 👤**

* Set up your project in ARCHIMEDES before initiating any data transfer
* Confirmed you are on the approved PI list from UOHI
* Create your [ARCHIMEDES account](https://archimedes.loris.ca/?page=request)
* Create a new project 
* Complete and submit the Data Contribution Form (DCF)
* Wait for DACO approval
* Create your project in ARCHIMEDES by providing all required information

➡️ **Step 2: Data Request & Review (ARCHIMEDES → UOHI) 📨**

* ARCHIMEDES initiates the data request based on your project information
* ARCHIMEDES submits the Data Request Form to the UOHI Privacy Office
* The Privacy Office reviews the request
* Once approved, you will be contacted by the Privacy team to grant access to your data location

➡️ **Step 3: De-identification (UOHI) 🔐**

* Your data is reviewed and prepared to meet privacy requirements
* De-identification is processed from the granted access  data storage location
* Your data is reviewed by the appropriate team:
    * Imaging data → UOHI IT
    * Behavioral / clinical data/ non imaging data → UOHI Privacy Team
* A final verification ensures all data is properly de-identified

➡️ **Step 4: Data Transfer & Ingestion in ARCHIMEDES 📤**

* Your data is securely transferred and made available in ARCHIMEDES
* Approved data are uploaded to the designated shared drive either by UOHI IT, UOHI Privacy, or the UOHI ARCHIMEDES team)
* ARCHIMEDES accesses the data directly (no duplication required)
* Data is ingested according to your project specifications
